Vocabulary for the representation of budget information and budget execution according to municipal management data: Description back to Index

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the information on budget and budget execution according to municipal management data:



This vocabulary is comprised of those concepts related to the budget projects, its execution and its corresponding liquidation. The concept [espresup:Presupuesto](#Presupuesto) represents the general features of a budget project: its approval date, if the budget has been extended, and its fiscal year. In this way we can represent not only the budget of the current fiscal year, but also the budget of previous years and the budget project for the next fiscal year. The budget is related to the general concept [Liquidacion](#Liquidacion) which represents the budget result. The budget is related with the propety [espresup:presupuestoIngreso](#presupuestoIngreso) to the budget income estimates, [espresup:PresupuestoIngreso](#PresupuestoIngreso) and with the property [espresup:presupuestoGasto](#presupuestoGasto) to the budget credits, [espresup:PresupuestoGasto](#PresupuestoGasto). The income corresponds to the initial estimates, and the expenses to the initial credits. The expenses can (optionally) be sustainable financial investments, [espresup:InversionFinancieramenteSostenible](#InversionFinancieramenteSostenible) that contain specific properties such as an id, a title, information on if its scope is all of the districts or one or several specific districts. Each income is related with the property [espresup:ejecucionIngreso](#ejecucionIngreso) with the income execution [espresup:EjecucionIngreso](#EjecucionIngreso) that has been generated during the fiscal year. Similarly, each expense is related with the property [espresup:ejecucionGasto](#ejecucionGasto) with the budget execution, [espresup:EjecucionGasto](#ejecucionGasto). Each execution relates through the property [espresup:periodoEjecucion](#periodoEjecucion) to an execution period which has been represented as a time interval using the class [time:ProperInterval](#time:ProperInterval) from the [Time Ontology](https://www.w3.org/TR/owl-time/). The income execution contains all the properties related with the execution stages. Similarly, the expense execution contains all the properties related to the stages of the expense execution. The income liquidation [espresup:LiquidacionIngreso](#LiquidacionIngreso) corresponds to the last income budget execution of the year an contains the excess or defect of the budget estimate. The same can be said of the expense execution, [espresup:LiquidacionGasto](#LiquidacionGasto) that contains the remaining credit. Figure 2 shows the conceptual model that represents a sustainable financial investment of a city council. A sustainable financial investment may have its own investment classification, [espresup:clasificacionInversion](#clasificacionInversion) and it is related to one or several budget expenses. Additionally, in this vocabulary SKOS taxonomies have been used to represent the organic classification for income and expenses, [espresup:clasificacionOrganica](#clasificacionOrganica), the expense economic classification [espresup:clasificacionEconomicaGasto](#clasificacionEconomicaGasto) and similarly the one for the incomey [espresup:clasificacionEconomicaIngreso](#clasificacionEconomicaIngreso), and the classification by expense programs, [espresup:clasificacionPrograma](#clasificacionPrograma). Figure 1 shows several relationships with the class of SKOS concepts. Each class skos:Concept corresponds to a specific taxonomy and its name is indicated between "<< >>". The economic and expense program classifications are "open", that is, the municipalities may create additional levels in the clasification. For example, in the case of the clasification by expense programs, the classification is "closed" in the first three levels, then in the next levels it is optional for each city council. Thus, each city council can create new groups of programs, in this case there is a SKOS for the regulated by law classification called clasificacion-programa-gasto, and another SKOS for the specific classification of the city council, clasificacion-programa-gasto-ayuntamiento. These two SKOS are related to indicate for example that a new program created by the city council is related o one of the groups of programs established by law.

Following we will show some examples of SKOS that have been created for the classifications by law and those that are specific to each city council. First, for the classification by expense program, we have developed a SKOS that contains for example the group of programs "231" that is a SKOS concept that belongs to the taxonomy of expense programs and that corresponds to "Primary social assistance". It has a relation "broader", that is, to a more general concept, the expense policy "23" of "Social services and social promotion" and this concept in turn is related with its expense area "2" of "Actuations for social protecion and promotion".
